The Best Movies According to Niharika

My top 3 movies of all-time.

Lagaan: Once Upon a Time in India (2001)

Directed by Ashutosh Gowariker, "Lagaan" is a period sports drama set during the British Raj in India. The film follows the story of Bhuvan (played by Aamir Khan), a young farmer who leads his fellow villagers in a cricket match against the oppressive British rulers. The film beautifully combines elements of sports, drama, and patriotism. "Lagaan" received widespread international acclaim and was even nominated for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film.

Dangal (2016)

Directed by Nitesh Tiwari, "Dangal" is a biographical sports drama based on the real-life story of wrestler Mahavir Singh Phogat and his daughters, Geeta and Babita Phogat. Aamir Khan, who portrays Mahavir Singh Phogat, underwent a remarkable physical transformation for his role. The film not only addresses themes of gender equality but also showcases the incredible journey of the Phogat sisters in the male-dominated world of wrestling. "Dangal" became one of the highest-grossing Indian films and received critical acclaim for its performances, storytelling, and impactful social message.

3 Idiots (2009)

Directed by Rajkumar Hirani, "3 Idiots" is a coming-of-age comedy-drama that revolves around the lives of three engineering students at an Indian engineering college. Starring Aamir Khan, R. Madhavan, and Sharman Joshi, the film explores the pressures and expectations of the education system and society. It delivers a powerful message about pursuing one's passion and embracing a non-traditional approach to education. With a perfect blend of humor, emotion, and social commentary, "3 Idiots" became a massive commercial success and resonated with audiences of all ages.
